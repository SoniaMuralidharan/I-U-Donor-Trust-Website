Corporate Employee Helps Urban Youth Become Organ Donors Via FB

A lot of people are working to get the families of brain-dead patients to donate their organs, save more lives. E.Senthil Kumar is a little different. With a ‘catch them young’ policy, the admin manager in a corporate firm in Parrys has gotten more than 110 youngsters to sign up for organ donation — using a specifically designed page on Facebook Called I&U Donor Trust, the seed to do something to increase organ donation started 8 years ago, “As part of my diploma course, I was required to work in a housekeeping department for a while. As luck would have it, I joined Apollo Hospitals in Greams Road and for the first time I got to see how doctors and patients worked. I was really having a nice time keeping patients comfortable, when one day I saw a patient who seemed fine in the afternoon, die a few hours later in front of me. He was healthy and his organs could have helped people but no one was there to tell his family about it. That’s when I decided to do something,” he explained.

Donor


A blood donation camp was organized by students of GKM College of Engineering & Technology, Department of Mechanical Engineering on August 14th 2014. It was initiated by the volunteers of S.Y.L.A (Share Your Love And Affection) . Professors Mr.Ram Kannan and Mr.Govindswamy encouraged the students and supported the camp . The camp was held at the college campus. The main associates were S.Y.L.A and I&U Donor Trust. The blood bank partners were Lions Club International Blood Bank, Egmore. The camp was successfully held with the help of volunteers of S.Y.L.A , founder of I&U donor trust Mr.E.Senthil kumar and NCC students of the college.The total participation was around 500 and the eligible donor count was 288. The students co- ordinator was C.Prabhu Srinivasan. During the event the Go RED momento was introduced and presented to the Governor of Lions Club International and to the Principal of GKMCET on behalf of I&u donor trust. The camp started at 10.30 AM and went on till 3.30 PM peacefully with the total co-operation of the students leaving an impact to the society to save lives by donating blood.
